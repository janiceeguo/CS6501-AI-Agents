Think of it like a coin toss: the outcome of the toss is uncertain and can be either heads or tails. If we were to repeat the toss many times, we might get different outcomes each time. That's essentially what a stochastic process is â€“ a sequence of random events that can be unpredictable.

Stochastic processes can be used to model a wide range of phenomena, including financial markets, population growth, and climate change. They're also used in many real-world applications, such as insurance claims, medical research, and financial modeling.
